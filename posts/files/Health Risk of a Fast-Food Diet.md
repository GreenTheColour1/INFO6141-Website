Increased consumption of fast food is linked to various chronic diseases, including obesity, diabetes, and heart diseases.

<br/>

## Obesity

<br/>

Fast food leads to weight gain primarily through high calorie intake; excessive fat, sugar, and nutrient-less food intake results in a daily calorie surplus. High-density fast food combined with less physical activity is resulting in a high body mass index (BMI). These foods are also designed to be highly palatable, which can lead to overeating and poor portion control (Nardocci et al., 2019).

<br/>

**Causes:**
- High calorie and sugar with fat combo

<br/>

## Type 2 Diabetes

<br/>

A high intake of fast food and ultra-processed food can increase the risk of Type 2 diabetes. Fast food mainly contains large amounts of refined carbohydrates and added sugars, which cause rapid spikes in blood glucose levels. Over time, this spike can lead to insulin resistance, a key factor for developing Diabetes. Additionally, obesity is a major risk factor for Type 2 Diabetes.

<br/>

**Causes:**
- High sugar intake
- Refined carbohydrate
- Insulin resistance
- Obesity

<br/>

## Cardiovascular Disease & Hypertension

<br/>

Study reveals that living in areas with higher access to fast food is associated with higher rates of hypertension and cardiovascular disease (Stevenson et al., 2023). Fast foods having high sodium levels contribute to high blood pressure. The study on Canadian adults found that neighbourhoods with more fast-food outlets showed a measurable increase in hypertension prevalence. In addition, trans fats and saturated fats found in fast food can raise cholesterol levels, increasing the risk of heart disease over time (Stevenson et al., 2023).

<br/>

**Causes:**
- High sodium intake
- Saturated and trans fats
- Increased cholesterol
- Environmental exposure (availability)

<br/>

## Cancer

<br/>

High consumption of ultra-processed foods has been linked to an increased risk of Cancer. A systematic review and meta-analysis found that diets rich in processed foods may contribute to cancer development due to additives, preservatives, and low nutritional quality. Additionally, these foods can promote obesity, which is itself a known risk factor for several types of cancer. Long-term dietary patterns play a crucial role in influencing cancer risk (Isaksen & Dankel, 2023).

<br/>

**Causes:**
- Food additives and preservatives
- Low nutritional value
- Obesity-related risk
- Chronic inflammation
